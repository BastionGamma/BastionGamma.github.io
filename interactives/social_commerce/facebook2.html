Facebook — Shoppable Pages

While the Shop Now button is featured prominently in both desktop and mobile versions, the Shop link is relegated to the left-side menu, where it appears in a lengthy list of options.

These shoppable pages are accessed through a link titled Shop. However, unlike the Shop Now button, brings the user to a store embedded in the Facebook page.

The stark difference in the adoption rates of shoppable pages on Facebook and Instagram highlights the importance of visibility. Only 19% of analyzed brands have adopted shoppable Facebook brand pages due to their limited number of available features and dismal navigation experience.

Brands that do not build out their own shoppable page embedded in Facebook must find a vendor compatible with both Facebook and the brand, making it difficult for many brands to implement the shoppable page functionality.

Moncler uses the Facebook interface to feature a list of items from its catalog, but users still need to click through to the brand site to complete a purchase.
